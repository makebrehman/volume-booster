Your concept is now clear enough to define a proper product direction. The app you are describing is essentially:

“A lightweight Android audio amplification utility focused on instant loudness enhancement through a minimal overlay-based control system.”

What you are building is not an equalizer app.
Not an audio studio app.
Not a media player.

It is a: “one-tap volume amplification layer.”

That simplicity is actually commercially strong because most users do not want DSP controls and frequency sliders. They want: “make my phone louder instantly.”

Your app structure would look like this:

User installs app → taps icon → bottom-sheet overlay appears → user adjusts:

System Volume

Boost Percentage


Then:

foreground service activates

persistent notification appears

boost remains active globally

tapping notification reopens same popup

tapping outside popup dismisses overlay


No login. No account. No cloud. No unnecessary settings.

That is a very good utility-app philosophy for Play Store distribution.

Now the technically important part:

Your requested behavior: “boost everything” including:

music

movies

calls

Bluetooth

headphones

speaker audio


is partially achievable but with limitations imposed by Android and hardware vendors.

Realistically:

You CAN globally boost:

media audio

most app sounds

speaker output

many Bluetooth outputs

wired headphones


You CANNOT reliably boost:

protected voice-call audio

some OEM-restricted streams

some Bluetooth hardware-limited outputs on every device.


So your Play Store description should say: “Compatible with most media and audio playback scenarios depending on device support.”

Because Android fragmentation is real.

Now the most important product architecture decision:

Your app should internally use:

1. Foreground Service Keeps audio effect alive continuously.


2. LoudnessEnhancer API Primary amplification engine.


3. AudioManager Controls actual Android media volume.


4. Overlay Window For popup bottom-sheet system.


5. MediaSession monitoring To maintain compatibility with media playback apps.


6. Device-aware gain limiting Very important for Play Store reviews.



Without limiter:

distortion becomes horrible

speakers may crackle

users give bad ratings


You need: “Safe Boost Management”

Example: 0–30% = safe 30–60% = warning 60–100% = risky
